Resume

In 2013, I began working for large corporations exclusively as a front-end programmer and designer. The difference between front-end programming and small business freelancing is quite huge. As a matter of fact, the only thing comparable is the communication skills necessary to do both types of jobs. The technology between the two is very different. Still, having worked for these large companies like Comcast, Blockbuster, Dish Network, and smaller companies like Eberls, Autopay, Envysion, Revelex, and Tag gave me a breadth of understanding across the technology landscape that I could never convey in a small paragraph.

Overall, I have been developing in the front end side of websites for 15 years now. I am very comfortable with modern day front end paradigms like CSS pre-processors, unit testing, task management systems, package management, version control systems, project management systems, automation etc. I have experience writing in vanilla javascript(though it is not my preference), including ECMA script 5 and 6, as well as in libraries like typescript. I can write up HTML pages quickly from designs or start from scratch building my own designs and styles as well as re-skinning existing corporate level sites.

My development practices have evolved to become modular, succinct, clean, easy to follow, and extend, semantic, and I always try to think about the developers who will have to come behind me and support the code in the future.

As far as architecture, I establish a clean, simple, best practices approach which facilitates in easy on-boarding of new developers, fast development, and ease of extending code.

My claims to fame are being able to jump into application stacks in companies and be contributing within days, having a good understanding for all around technology, being able to troubleshoot and diagnose as well as solve issues that may arise from combined systems. I can come up with creative solutions to complex problems. Also, I have been known to take hundreds of lines of code and reduce them to several lines quite often, finding the cleanest and most succinct methodologies every time I look at code.

An average day in a corporate environment has me working on several projects, assisting other developers with their work, architecting in all aspects of development on the front side, and assisting in managing repos, servers, automation, security, QA efforts, writing tests, best practices, documentation, etc.
